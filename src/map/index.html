Bitcoin Production by Country

There are a number of ways to break down the global energy consumption of Bitcoin, in order to gain a more precise and clear understanding of that consumption. Perhaps the most obvious category to divide Bitcoin’s consumption into is by country, as that will give us a physical sense for where geographically the most energy is being consumed for Bitcoin. The following visualization shows, from September 2019 to April 2020, which countries produced the largest amount of Bitcoin. Specifically, we are measuring each country's percentage share of the Bitcoin hashrate for that month. This visualization shows us where Bitcoin production (and thus energy consumption) was concentrated during that time, and how that changed over time.

It is worth noting that the dataset for this visualization can only give us a limited understanding of the geographical breakdown of Bitcoin production, due to both its limited range and its age. For example, the visualization clearly shows China as by far the largest producer of Bitcoin, ranging from 65-75% of the monthly hashrate share. However, we know that this is no longer the case, as China’s 2021 ban on cryptocurrencies has caused a sharp decline in mining activities there, and currently the US holds the greatest number of Bitcoin miners. However, there are still some points of interest to take from this map: first, that Bitcoin mining is widespread, with countries in every part of the map contributing (albeit usually in very small amounts) to Bitcoin production. Additionally, looking at the changes to the graph over time we can see that Bitcoin production is also growing across the world, as more countries start contributing to the Bitcoin hashrate share, and China’s majority share is lessened by 10% between September and April. All of this points to an industry that is growing increasingly popular, not just in the largest and most consumptive nations like China and the US, but also throughout the entire world.
